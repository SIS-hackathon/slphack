  1. Students

    First we will ask about the students in your household. They are the ones that would potentially receive free or reduced price school meals.

  2. Add the First Student You are Applying For

    Students are those in grades pre-kindergarten through grade 12.


       

  3. Is a Foster Child?

    A foster child is a child who is formally placed by a court or a state child welfare agency. Any students who are foster children can get free school meals.


       

  4. Is Any of the Below?

    Any students with one or more of the statuses below can get free school meals.


    If no, please press the "CONTINUE" button.
       

  5. Has Been Added to the Application

    Any Other Students also Applying?

    Please add all students in your household who go to schools in this school district.


       

  6. Other Benefits Programs

    Next we will ask you if anyone in the household participates in other benefits programs. Participating in certain benefits programs makes all students in the household eligible to get free school meals.

       

  7. Does anyone in the household participate in one or more of the benefits programs below?

    Someone in the household participating in one or more of the below means all students in your household can get free school meals. If yes, please select a program and provide a case number if there is one.

    If no, please press the "CONTINUE" button.
       

  8. Adults and Adult Income

    Next we will ask you about any adults in the household and the income they have.

       

  9. Adults in the Household

    Please add anyone 18 years or older in your household. If you added a student that is 18 years or older already, do not add that student again here.



       

  10. Social Security Number Information

    Please provide the last 4 digits of your Social Security Number. If you do not have a Social Security Number, you can provide the last 4 digits of the Social Security Number of another adult in the household or check the box below.


       

  11. Other Children and Child Income

    Next we will ask you about any other children in the household that are not students. Then we will ask about any income that both students and other children in the household have.

       

  12. Other Children in the Household

    Please add any other children (less than 18 years old) in the household who are not students or are students in other school districts. If you added a child as a student already, do not add that student again here.


     
       

  13. Do any students or other children have income?

    Click "set income" below to add income for any student or other child

       

  14. Provide Contact Details

    Next we'll ask for the best ways to contact you plus a few more questions to finish your application.

       

  15. Please Provide Your Contact Details

    Your contact information will help the school or school district using this application get in touch with you. You are not required to provide all of the contact details below.

    Use of Information Statement

    “The Richard B. Russell National School Lunch Act requires the information on this application. You do not have to give the information, but if you do not submit all needed information, we cannot approve your child for free or reduced price meals. You must include the last four digits of the social security number of the adult household member who signs the application. The social security number is not required when you apply on behalf of a foster child or you list a Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP), Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF) Program or Food Distribution Program on Indian Reservations (FDPIR) case number or other FDPIR identifier for your child or when you indicate that the adult household member signing the application does not have a social security number. We will use your information to determine if your child is eligible for free or reduced price meals, and for administration and enforcement of the lunch and breakfast programs.

    We may share your eligibility information with education, health, and nutrition programs to help them evaluate, fund, or determine benefits for their programs, auditors for program reviews, and law enforcement officials to help them look into violations of program rules.”
